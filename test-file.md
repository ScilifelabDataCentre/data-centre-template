# Regulations

This is not a comprehensive document of regulatory requirements, and further requirements might apply to specific
applications. The following are some of the most important regulations which we need to consider when developing software:

## GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

[A developer's guide to GDPR from IBM](https://developer.ibm.com/articles/s-gdpr1/)

GDPR does not mean we cannot collect personal data, it means we should not collect more data than is _necessary_.

As [verksamt.se](https://verksamt.se/en/agreement-invoicing/personal-data) says:

> All these rules, reasons and other legal text of the regulation are based on a few basic principles and you will get
> far by just following these three points:
>
> - Do not collect more personal data than is necessary and only for a specific, predetermined purpose.
> - Inform about what data you save and do not save it longer than necessary.
> - Protect the personal data you handle in the company.

[6 steps to comply with GDPR from IMY (Swedish authority on privacy protection)](https://www.imy.se/verksamhet/dataskydd/innovationsportalen/ar-det-ni-tankt-gora-ar-forenligt-med-gdpr/)

## European Accessibility Act (EAA)

The EAA requires digital services, including university software, to be accessible; see [Accessibility docs](accessibility.md)
for details.

## Regulations for state agencies/statliga myndigheter

Universities are state agencies and thus Swedish Civil Contingencies Agency’s (MSB) directives apply by law:

- Information security in governmental authorities ([MSBFS 2020:6](https://www.msb.se/sv/regler/gallande-regler/msbfs-20206/))
- Security measures in information systems for governmental authorities ([MSBFS 2020:7](https://www.msb.se/sv/regler/gallande-regler/msbfs-20207/))
- Reporting IT incidents for governmental authorities ([MSBFS 2020:8](https://www.msb.se/sv/regler/gallande-regler/foreskrifter-om-rapportering-av-it-incidenter-for-statliga-myndigheter-msbfs-20208/)).

Each university then uses these to derive their own concrete procedures, for example [Uppsala University](https://www.uu.se/en/staff/organisation-and-governance/regulations/information-security-procedures). As they
are all derived from the same parent directives, the fundamentals should be the same. These derived university
guidelines are more succinct and concrete than the MSB documents.
